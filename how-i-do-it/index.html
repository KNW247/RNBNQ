How I Do It — Real Brewing. Real Gear. My Way.

This isn’t the way. It might not even be the best way. It’s just my way — how I brew, clean, and build things with the gear I have and the space I’m in.

This series walks through how I approach different parts of the brewing process, solve problems, and make it work in the real world.

I’ll sometimes shout out gear or producers who support the homebrewing community and the clubs I’m part of. These aren’t paid promotions — just genuine nods to folks doing right by brewers.

Ep. 1 – How I Clean My Fermenter (CIP Build)

My DIY CIP system uses a ¾ HP, 3300 GPH pump for industrial-level cleaning. No lifting, just fast, effective results.

In this video, I walk through exactly how I clean my fermenter using my DIY CIP (Clean-in-Place) setup.

The heart of this system is a ¾ HP, 3300 GPH pump—delivering 4–6× the flow rate of most homebrew CIP kits. It’s industrial-level cleaning power, built for a homebrew budget.

This isn’t a parts list or theory—this is how I actually clean my gear. No heavy lifting, Just fast, effective cleaning that gets me back to brewing.
