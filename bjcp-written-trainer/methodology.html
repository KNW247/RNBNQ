What This Trainer Teaches

Strong written exam recipe answers are not random ingredient lists. They are coherent beer designs built from connected decisions.

  • Build gravity targets from strength and finish goals
  • Set bitterness from gravity and style balance
  • Estimate grist requirements quickly and consistently
  • Select mash schedules that support body and attenuation goals
  • Select fermentation profiles that match style intent
  • Compare similar styles using exam-ready language

Trainer Assumptions

  • Metric recipe construction
  • 21.5 liters into fermenter
  • 70% brewhouse efficiency
  • ABV targets in 0.25% increments
  • Exam-speed approximation over software precision

The Gravity Shortcut

ABV × 8 = approximate gravity drop

Example: 5.0% ABV × 8 = 40 gravity points

Expected FG 1.010 → Estimated OG 1.050

Fast, repeatable, and close enough for exam construction.

Why Balance Matters

The written exam rewards coherent beer design, not isolated memorized numbers.

A German Pils should carry firmer bitterness than a Munich Helles. A Märzen should present lower bitterness relative to gravity. A Sweet Stout needs enough bitterness to offset residual sweetness.

This trainer teaches bitterness as recipe structure, not trivia.
